# Chart Patterns

These patterns are the default vocabulary for chart planning in `awesome-presentation`.

## Investment Research / Market Analysis

- KPI strip: price, valuation, yield, growth rate, revision count, market cap, or spread.
- Yield curve: maturity on x-axis, yield on y-axis, labeled latest date/source.
- Scenario matrix: base/upside/downside with triggers, expected impact, and monitoring signals.
- Heatmap: sector/risk/region sensitivity.
- Waterfall: drivers of margin, revenue, cash flow, rate move, or valuation change.

## Fundraising / Product Pitch

- Funnel: awareness to activation to paid conversion, or sales pipeline.
- Timeline: product roadmap, GTM sequence, milestones.
- Quadrant: competitor positioning or buyer pain vs urgency.
- KPI strip: traction, retention, sales cycle, ACV, deployment time.
- Process flow: product workflow or before/after operating model.

## Executive Report / Strategy

- Scenario matrix: choices, risks, mitigations, decision points.
- Heatmap: business unit x capability, risk x likelihood, segment x attractiveness.
- Waterfall: plan vs actual, budget bridge, initiative contribution.
- Timeline: rollout plan and governance milestones.
- Quadrant: priority map or strategic tradeoff.

## Teaching / Explainer

- Concept map: parts of a system and relationships.
- Timeline: sequence of discovery, process, or lifecycle.
- Comparison matrix: old vs new, concept A vs concept B.
- Step flow: worked example or procedure.
- KPI strip only when numbers help memory.

## Data-Poor Decks

If no trustworthy numbers are available, choose:

- Concept diagram
- Process flow
- Comparison matrix
- Timeline with user-provided or clearly generic stages
- Scenario matrix with assumptions labeled

Avoid numeric precision when the source material does not support it.
